In 1917 an extraordinary woman who was extremely apt at recognizing patterns changed the face of cryptology and coding forever. Known as “The Woman Who Smashed Codes” Elizabeth Smith (later Friedman) cracked all codes imaginable: from Japanese Naval codes during World War One to codes penned by criminals and later Nazi messages. Her contributions to code breaking led to the US army hiring thousands of women to decrypt codes – these became known as Pencil Pushin’ Mamas.

Inspired by Elizabeth and the many women in code breaking we created this website: an homage to all the amazing women in tech throughout history.

Coded by us in line with our Code First: Girls coding workshops we are Inbal and Noelle and we hope this website gives you an insight into all the unheard voices in tech – they are certainly women that inspired us and kicked some ass!
